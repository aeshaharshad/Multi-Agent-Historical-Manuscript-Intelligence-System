Napoleon Bonaparte was a French military leader who rose to prominence during the French
Revolution. Born in 1769 on the island of Corsica, he became Emperor of the French in
1804 after crowning himself at Notre-Dame Cathedral in Paris.

In 1805, Napoleon achieved one of his greatest victories at the Battle of Austerlitz,
defeating the combined armies of Emperor Francis II of Austria and Tsar Alexander I of
Russia. The battle took place in Austerlitz, in modern-day Czech Republic, and is often
called the Battle of the Three Emperors.

In 1812, Napoleon launched his disastrous invasion of Russia. His Grande Armée reached
Moscow but was forced to retreat after the city was burned. The retreat through the
Russian winter destroyed most of his army.

After his defeat at the Battle of Leipzig in 1813 by a coalition of Prussia, Austria,
Russia, and Sweden, Napoleon was forced to abdicate in 1814 and was exiled to the island
of Elba. He escaped in 1815 and returned to France, beginning the Hundred Days.

His final defeat came at the Battle of Waterloo in 1815, where he was defeated by
the Duke of Wellington and Field Marshal Blücher. He was exiled again, this time to
Saint Helena in the South Atlantic, where he died in 1821.
